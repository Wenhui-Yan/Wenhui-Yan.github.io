I am a first-year M.S. student in Land Use and Information Technology at China Agricultural University, advised by Yelu Zeng. My research interests lie in quantitative remote sensing of vegetation, particularly in leveraging solar-induced chlorophyll fluorescence (SIF) to understand vegetation physiological responses and drought dynamics. I am broadly interested in advancing remote sensing methods for monitoring ecosystem stress and climate impacts.

I received my Bachelor's degree in Geographic Information Science from China Agricultural University, where I also worked under the supervision of Yelu Zeng.

    Publications

    Escape Ratio Contributes More Than Fluorescence Yield to SIF GPP Relationship Over Crops and Rainforest

    Wenhui Yan, Yelu Zeng*, Xinhong Zhang, Weike Zhao, Yongyuan Gao, Yaohang He, Dalei Hao*

    IEEE Geoscience and Remote Sensing Letters, 2024

    webpage | pdf |

    Solar-induced chlorophyll fluorescence (SIF) is an effective indicator for tracking gross primary productivity (GPP), but the relative roles of canopy structure and physiology remain unclear. This study evaluates the contributions of photon escape ratio and fluorescence yield to the SIF-GPP relationship across crops and rainforest using multiple satellite products. Results show that structure-related escape ratio better captures light use efficiency variation than fluorescence yield, highlighting its strong potential for monitoring GPP changes within and across biomes.

    Tree-level carbon stock estimations across diverse species using multi-source remote sensing integration

    Li Qing, Jia Yin, Xiaoxin Zhang, Dalei Hao, Matheus Pinheiro Ferreira, Wenhui Yan, Ye Tian, Da Zhang, Shen Tan, Shengn Nie, Tianyu An, Xiaoyao Li, Jianxi Huang, Wei Su, Yelu Zeng*

    Computers and Electronics in Agriculture, 2025

    webpage | pdf |

    This study integrates UAV LiDAR and high-resolution optical imagery to estimate individual-tree biomass and carbon stock across species. The method improves accuracy through species-specific modeling and by combining structural variables with vegetation indices. It reports substantial gains over single-source and non-species-specific approaches, showing strong potential for large-scale precise forest carbon monitoring.

    Mapping tree height in complex terrain of northern China using ultra-high- resolution images

    Qing Li, Xiaoxin Zhang, Dalei Hao, Wenhui Yan, Qi Zhao, Ye Tian, Yelu Zeng*

    Smart Agricultural Technology, 2025

    webpage | pdf |

    Focusing on complex mountainous terrain in northern China, this paper compares spectral-only and terrain-enhanced strategies for tree-height mapping from ultra-high-resolution imagery. Among tested models, XGBoost with spectral and topographic features performs best, producing a 3 m tree-height map with robust accuracy. The study also highlights limitations of existing global tree-height products in complex terrain.

    Global-PCG-10: a 10 m global map of plastic-covered greenhouses derived from Sentinel-2 in 2020

    Bowen Niu, Quanlong Feng*, Bingwen Qiu, Shuai Su, Xinmin Zhang, Rongji Cui, Xinhong Zhang, Fanli Sun, Wenhui Yan, Siyuan Zhao, Hanyu Shi, Cong Ou, Xiaolu Yan, Jianhua Gong, Gaofei Yin, Jianxi Huang, Jiantao Liu, Bingbo Gao, Xiaochuang Yao, Jianyu Yang, and Dehai Zhu

    Earth System Science Data, 2025

    webpage | pdf |

    This paper presents Global-PCG-10, a 10 m global dataset of plastic-covered greenhouses built from Sentinel-2 with deep learning and active-learning label refinement. The resulting map reaches high overall accuracy and quantifies global PCG distribution patterns, with strong concentration in Asia and especially China. The dataset also reveals systematic misclassification of PCGs in existing global land-cover products.
